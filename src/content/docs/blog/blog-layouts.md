Your blog archive (the page that lists your posts) can use a grid or list layout. All layout settings are in the Customizer.

## Before you begin

- Publish a few blog posts so you can preview layout changes.
- Open **Appearance → Customize → Blog** in your WordPress admin.

## Choose an archive layout

1. In the Customizer, open the **Blog Archive** section.
2. Under **Layout**, pick one:
   - **Grid** — posts in equal-sized cards arranged in a responsive grid.
   - **List** — posts in a vertical list with the featured image on the side.

3. If you picked **Grid**, set the **Columns** to 2, 3, or 4.

The preview updates as you change settings. Click **Publish** to save.

## Set the sidebar

Under **Blog Archive**, find the **Sidebar** option and choose:

- **Right** — sidebar on the right, content on the left.
- **Left** — sidebar on the left, content on the right.
- **None** — full width, no sidebar.

## Configure post cards

Post cards show a preview of each post on the archive page.

- **Thumbnail ratio** — choose the aspect ratio for featured images: 1:1 (square), 4:3, 16:9, or original.
- **Excerpt length** — set how many words appear in the post summary.

## Choose pagination style

Under **Pagination**, pick one:

- **Numbers** — standard page-number links at the bottom.
- **Load more** — a **Load More** button loads the next set of posts.
- **Infinite scroll** — new posts load automatically as you scroll down.

## Set up the blog header

The blog header appears at the top of the archive page.

1. Open the **Blog Header** section in the Customizer.
2. Turn on **Enable Blog Header**.
3. Set the background image, overlay color and opacity, height, and text colors.

The preview updates as you make changes.
