title: Use Elementor page templates
description: Start from a pre-built layout instead of a blank page when editing with Elementor.
---

Elementor includes a library of page templates you can insert into any page. Milano ships with additional templates designed for WooCommerce stores. Using a template saves you from building every page from scratch.

## Insert a template into a page

1. Go to **Pages → Add New** (or edit an existing page).
2. Click **Edit with Elementor**.
3. Click the **folder icon** in the content area to open the template library.
4. Browse or search for a template that fits your needs.
5. Hover over a template and click **Insert**.

Elementor replaces the page content with the template. You can then edit text, images, and sections to match your store.

:::note
Inserting a template replaces the current page content. If you already have content on the page, save it first or insert the template into a new page.
:::

## Milano-specific templates

Milano includes templates for common store pages:

- **Homepage layouts** — hero sections, featured products, and category grids.
- **About page** — team sections, brand story, and values.
- **Contact page** — contact form, map, and store hours.
- **Product showcase** — grid or carousel layouts for featured items.

These templates appear in the Elementor library under the **My Templates** or **Milano** tab, depending on how they are bundled.

## Save your own templates

You can save a page you built as a reusable template.

1. Build your page in Elementor.
2. Click the arrow next to **Update** at the bottom of the panel.
3. Select **Save as template**.
4. Give your template a name and click **Save**.

Your template now appears in the library for use on any page.

## Export and import templates

You can share templates between sites or back them up.

### Export a template

1. Go to **Templates → Saved Templates** in your WordPress dashboard.
2. Hover over the template you want to export.
3. Click **Export Template**.
4. A `.json` file downloads to your computer.

### Import a template

1. Go to **Templates → Saved Templates**.
2. Click **Import Templates** at the top.
3. Select the `.json` file from your computer.
4. Click **Import now**.

The template appears in your library and is ready to insert.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The template library does not load.
**Fix:** Check your internet connection. The library loads from Elementor's servers. If the problem persists, clear your browser cache.

**Problem:** Milano templates do not appear in the library.
**Fix:** Make sure Milano and its companion plugins are installed and active. Go to **Milano → Dashboard** and check that all required plugins are installed.

**Problem:** A template looks wrong after inserting it.
**Fix:** The template may use fonts or colors defined in Milano's settings. Go to **Milano → Theme Settings** and confirm your typography and color settings match the demo.
