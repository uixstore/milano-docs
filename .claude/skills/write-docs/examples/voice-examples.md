# Voice — before / after

Concrete rewrites that show the Milano voice in action. When drafting, pattern-match against these. When reviewing, use them to justify edits.

Each pair is labeled with the **rule that fired**. If two rules fire on one example, both are listed.

---

## Lead with the action, not the preamble

> **Rule:** First sentence of a section does the work. No throat-clearing.

❌ If you'd like to change the logo on your site, the first thing you'll need to do is navigate over to the Customizer panel which can be found under the Appearance menu.

✅ To change your logo, go to **Appearance → Customize → Site Identity**.

---

❌ Now that you've installed Milano, you're probably wondering what to do next. Well, the next thing on your list should be registering your purchase code.

✅ Next, register your purchase code so Milano can check for updates.

---

❌ In this section, we're going to take a look at how the header builder works and walk through some of its features.

✅ The header builder lets you drag elements into the header layout. Here's how it works.

---

## Cut filler phrases

> **Rule:** "in order to" → "to". "at this point" → "now". "please note that" → drop. "the fact that" → drop. "it is important to" → drop.

❌ In order to save your changes, please note that you'll need to click the Publish button at this point.

✅ To save, click **Publish**.

---

❌ It is important to remember that you need to clear your cache after making changes to the Customizer.

✅ Clear your cache after changing Customizer settings — otherwise your site will look the same to visitors.

---

❌ At this point in time, Milano supports two page builders.

✅ Milano supports two page builders: Elementor and WPBakery.

---

## Banned words — "easy", "simple", "just"

> **Rule:** Never tell the reader something is easy. If it is, they'll feel it. If it isn't, you've insulted them.

❌ Importing a demo is easy — just click the Import button and you're done.

✅ To import a demo, click **Import** on the demo card. Milano installs the required plugins, downloads the content, and activates it.

---

❌ You can simply drag the widget into the sidebar.

✅ Drag the widget into the sidebar.

---

❌ Just head over to the Customizer and you'll see the option there.

✅ Open **Appearance → Customize**. The option appears under **Colors**.

---

## Banned words — "obviously", "of course", "merely"

> **Rule:** Patronizing. The reader doesn't know what's obvious to you.

❌ Of course, you'll want to back up your site before activating a new theme.

✅ Back up your site before activating a new theme.

---

❌ Obviously, the parent theme must be installed before the child theme will work.

✅ Install the parent theme before activating the child theme.

---

## Skip celebrations and hedges

> **Rule:** No "Awesome!", "Great job!", "You're all set!". No "you might want to consider…". Say "do X" or "we recommend X".

❌ Awesome! You've installed Milano. Great job! Now you might want to consider importing a demo to get started.

✅ Milano is installed. Next, import a demo or build your site from scratch.

---

❌ You may want to think about possibly enabling the sticky header for a better user experience.

✅ We recommend turning on the sticky header so the menu stays visible as visitors scroll.

---

## Second person, never "the user"

> **Rule:** Address the reader directly. "You", not "the user" or "users".

❌ The user can configure the header layout from the Customizer. Users will find the option under Header settings.

✅ You configure the header layout in the Customizer, under **Header settings**.

---

❌ If the user has WooCommerce installed, additional options appear.

✅ If you have WooCommerce installed, you'll see extra options.

---

## Active voice, present tense

> **Rule:** Active and present. Avoid passive constructions and future tense where present works.

❌ The settings will be saved when the Publish button is clicked.

✅ Click **Publish** to save.

---

❌ Once the file has been uploaded, it can be activated from the Themes screen.

✅ After you upload the file, activate it from the **Themes** screen.

---

## Short sentences — break anything past 25 words

> **Rule:** Aim 15–20 words. If a sentence runs past 25, split it. One idea per sentence.

❌ Milano includes a powerful header builder that lets you create custom header layouts by dragging and dropping elements into different positions, with support for multiple rows, sticky behavior, transparent backgrounds, and a separate mobile header that can be configured independently from the desktop version.

✅ The header builder lets you drag elements into header rows. You can set sticky or transparent behavior, and configure a separate mobile header.

---

## Plain-word swaps

> **Rule:** Prefer the shorter, common word. "utilize" → "use". "purchase" → "buy". "modify" → "change". "enable/disable" → "turn on/off" (in body copy; the UI label itself may say "Enable" — keep that bold as-is).

❌ You can utilize the child theme to modify the appearance without affecting the parent theme.

✅ Use the child theme to change the appearance without touching the parent theme.

---

❌ To purchase additional Milano licenses, visit ThemeForest.

✅ To buy more Milano licenses, visit ThemeForest.

---

❌ Click the toggle to enable the sticky header.

✅ Click the toggle to turn on the sticky header.

(Note: if the UI label itself reads "Enable sticky header", write: **Click **Enable sticky header**.** — keep the label verbatim.)

---

## Terminology lock-ins

> **Rule:** Customizer (not Theme Options). demo content (not sample data). menu (not nav). sidebar (not side panel). WordPress and WooCommerce spelled out in body.

❌ Open the Theme Options panel, then go to the Nav settings on the side panel.

✅ Open the **Customizer**, then go to **Menus** in the sidebar.

---

❌ Import the sample data to populate your site, then customize it for WP and Woo.

✅ Import the demo content to populate your site. The demos work with both WordPress and WooCommerce.

---

## UI labels are bold and verbatim

> **Rule:** Bold for any clickable thing. Match the label exactly — don't paraphrase.

❌ Click the save button at the top right.

✅ Click **Save Changes**.

---

❌ Go to the customize section under appearance.

✅ Go to **Appearance → Customize**.

---

## Inclusive language

> **Rule:** No "guys". Use "they" instead of "he/she" — or rewrite to "you". No idioms that don't translate.

❌ When one of your customers makes a purchase, he or she will receive an email confirmation.

✅ When a customer buys something, they receive an email confirmation.

---

❌ Hey guys, let's take a look at how to set up the cart page.

✅ Here's how to set up the cart page.

---

❌ Setting up Milano is a piece of cake.

✅ Setting up Milano takes about 30 minutes.

---

## Alt text — describe what's shown, not "Screenshot of…"

> **Rule:** Alt text describes the content, never the medium. Lead with the subject.

❌ `Screenshot of the Customizer`

✅ `Customizer panel with the brand-blue color selected for primary buttons`

---

❌ `Image showing the header builder`

✅ `Header builder with a logo on the left, menu in the center, and search icon on the right`

---

## No exclamation marks except in genuine warnings

> **Rule:** A warning is genuine when ignoring it breaks the site or loses data. Everything else is calm.

❌ Welcome to Milano! Get ready to build an awesome store!

✅ Welcome to Milano. This guide takes you from a fresh install to a working store in about 30 minutes.

---

❌ Don't forget to back up your site!

✅ :::caution
   Back up your site before activating a new theme.
   :::

---

## Admonitions — pick the right severity

> **Rule:** `:::note` neutral info · `:::tip` optional best practice · `:::caution` must-do or it won't work · `:::danger` could break the site or lose data.

❌ `:::note` Always back up your database before running an update — restoring from a broken state is painful. `:::`

✅ `:::danger` Back up your database before updating. A failed update can corrupt content beyond recovery. `:::`

---

❌ `:::danger` You can change the accent color in the Customizer. `:::`

✅ `:::note` You can change the accent color from **Appearance → Customize → Colors**. `:::`

---

## "Next step" links — descriptive, never "click here"

> **Rule:** Link text should make sense out of context. A screen reader user hearing "click here, click here, click here" gets nothing.

❌ Once you're done, click [here](../register-purchase-code/) to register your purchase code.

✅ Next: [Register your purchase code](../register-purchase-code/).

---

❌ For more info, click [this link](../../shop/wishlist/).

✅ Learn more in [Wishlist setup](../../shop/wishlist/).

---

## Numbers — spell out one–nine; numerals for 10+, version numbers, steps

> **Rule:** Step counts and version numbers are always numerals.

❌ Milano supports 3 page builders and requires WordPress version five point nine or higher.

✅ Milano supports three page builders and requires WordPress 5.9 or higher.

---

❌ Follow these eight steps to set up your store.

✅ Follow these 8 steps to set up your store.

---

## Define WP/Woo jargon on first use

> **Rule:** Audience is non-technical store owners. The first time a term appears on a page, define it inline.

❌ Configure the permalink structure to use post-name slugs.

✅ Configure the permalink structure (the format of your page URLs) to use post-name slugs — for example, `your-site.com/about/` instead of `your-site.com/?p=42`.

---

❌ Enable the REST API for the WooCommerce endpoints.

✅ For developers: the WooCommerce REST API endpoints can be enabled from **WooCommerce → Settings → Advanced → REST API**. (Most store owners don't need this.)

---

## The "what would a colleague say?" sniff test

When in doubt, ask: would a coworker actually say this sentence out loud while pointing at the screen? If not, rewrite.

❌ Subsequent to the activation of the theme, you will be prompted to install the requisite companion plugins.

✅ After you activate Milano, it asks you to install the required plugins.
